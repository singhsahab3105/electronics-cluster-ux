This study focuses on understanding how existing platforms
approach talent discovery, trust-building, and decision-making
for complex execution work.

## Platforms Analyzed

### 1. Upwork
- Open freelancer marketplace
- Skill-based search and filters
- Heavy reliance on ratings and earnings

**Observations**
- Too many choices create confusion
- Founders must define roles themselves
- Quality comparison is difficult

**Key Issue**
High cognitive load for non-HR founders

---

### 2. Toptal
- Highly curated expert network
- Entry via consultation call
- Strong screening narrative

**Observations**
- Trust is established early
- Users feel guided, not rushed
- Limited visibility but high confidence

**Key Strength**
Consult-first approach reduces risk perception

---

### 3. Fiverr Pro
- Packaged professional services
- Brand-backed professionals
- Fixed-scope offerings

**Observations**
- Easier entry than Upwork
- Less suitable for complex hardware work
- Execution ownership unclear

**Key Limitation**
Works better for tasks than full product execution

---

## Key Learnings

- Guidance before choice increases confidence
- Trust matters more than pricing
- Fewer options improve decision quality

## Design Takeaway

This platform avoids open marketplaces and instead
recommends execution models before showing options.
